# AI Disclosure

This paper was developed with substantial assistance from **Mavis** (an AI assistant from MiniMax, based on the M3 model). The use of AI is explicit and disclosed in §1 of the paper.

## How Mavis was used

Mavis contributed to the following aspects of the work:

1. **Physics literature review** — helping find and verify 22 cited papers (brane-world scenarios, dark matter observations, CMB constraints, black hole physics, dark energy equation of state, etc.)
2. **Numerical verification** — running Python calculations to verify every numerical claim (10^38 hierarchy, 10^-85 f_back, 5%/27%/68% energy budget, 2D universe lifetimes, black hole Schwarzschild radii, Hawking evaporation time, supernova energy conversion, Sun's total output, etc.)
3. **Internal consistency audits** — repeatedly reading the paper to identify cross-reference errors, numerical inconsistencies, contradictory claims, and overstatements
4. **Iterative refinement** — proposing framings (e.g., the "two dark-sector products" framing, the "universal bulk-brane cancellation" framing, the "ending-agnostic" framing) that the human author then accepted, modified, or rejected
5. **Prose editing** — making the writing clearer and more accessible to non-specialists
6. **Honesty checks** — pushing for explicit acknowledgment of what the model *does not* derive (inflation, baryogenesis, BBN, Standard Model parameters, neutrinos)

## What Mavis did *not* do

1. **Mavis did not invent the central idea.** The dimensional-cascade model — the 4D event projecting to 3+1D, the inversion principle, the 2D universe back-projection as dark matter, the universal bulk-brane cancellation — originated with the human author in conversation. Mavis helped *clarify* and *defend* these ideas, but did not propose the core framework.
2. **Mavis did not derive the math.** The paper is qualitative. The "Mathematical sketch" in §2.4 is a sign-and-order-of-magnitude argument, not a derivation. Mavis did not provide a Lagrangian, action, or equations of motion.
3. **Mavis did not write the paper autonomously.** Every section was reviewed and often rewritten by the human author. Many of Mavis's suggestions were rejected.
4. **Mavis did not fabricate references.** All 22 cited papers are real and verified.

## Honest assessment

The model is the human author's. Mavis was a tool — a *very* capable collaborator with a broad literature review and a strong instinct for internal consistency, but a tool nonetheless. The conceptual content, the central postulates (inversion principle, universal bulk-brane cancellation, ending-agnosticism), the testable predictions, the honest acknowledgment of gaps — these are the human author's.

The AI's role was to:
- Speed up literature review
- Catch numerical errors
- Catch internal inconsistencies
- Push for honesty about limitations

These are valuable, but they are *tool* contributions, not *author* contributions.

## Why disclose this prominently

- The arXiv 2026 rules require explicit AI disclosure.
- ai.viXra.org (March 2025) is a platform specifically for AI-assisted work; it requires disclosure.
- The regular viXra.org *rejects* AI-assisted work entirely.
- The LessWrong / HackerNews / academic communities increasingly expect AI disclosure.
- Intellectual honesty demands it: a reader should know whether the author is a domain expert, a curious outsider, or an AI.

This work is by **a curious outsider, assisted by an AI**. The combination has produced a coherent thought experiment, but it has not produced a theory of everything. The paper is honest about this.
