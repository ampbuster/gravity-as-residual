AGN Host Galaxy DM Test Results (MaNGA DR15)
============================================

Sample: MaNGA DR15 (10,220 galaxies, 9,705 with valid data)
DM indicator: M_dyn / M_star (Wolf+ 2010)
AGN indicator: log SFR(Halpha) (logSFRHa)
Cascade prediction: AGN/control = 1.05 (+5% from active contribution)
ΛCDM prediction: AGN/control ~ 1.00 (no correlation)

PER-GALAXY M/L ANALYSIS (matched M_star bins):

log M* = 9.5-10.0: N_AGN=  5, N_Ctrl=586, M/L = 0.15 vs 0.33 = 0.46 (-54%)
log M* = 10.0-10.3: N_AGN=  8, N_Ctrl=199, M/L = 0.76 vs 0.64 = 1.19 (+19%)
log M* = 10.3-10.5: N_AGN= 50, N_Ctrl=119, M/L = 0.56 vs 0.76 = 0.74 (-27%)
log M* = 10.5-10.7: N_AGN=118, N_Ctrl=126, M/L = 0.52 vs 0.99 = 0.53 (-47%)
log M* = 10.7-11.0: N_AGN=307, N_Ctrl=308, M/L = 0.77 vs 1.13 = 0.68 (-32%)
log M* = 11.0-11.3: N_AGN=347, N_Ctrl=349, M/L = 0.90 vs 1.26 = 0.71 (-29%)

INTERPRETATION:

The test is HEAVILY CONFOUNDED by morphology:
- High logSFRHa galaxies (whether AGN or strong SF) are predominantly
  LATE-TYPE (spiral, irregular)
- Late-type galaxies have INTRINSICALLY lower M_dyn/M_star at fixed M*
  (the morphology-DM relation)
- The "control" (low logSFRHa) is dominated by QUESCENT galaxies,
  which are mostly EARLY-TYPE
- Early-type galaxies have INTRINSICALLY higher M_dyn/M_star at fixed M*
- So the test mostly measures "late vs early type", not "AGN vs not AGN"

This is NOT a clean falsification of the cascade, because:
1. Many "AGN-like" galaxies are actually strong SF (not true AGN)
2. The morphology difference dominates the signal
3. A proper test needs BPT-classified AGN AND morphology matching

WHAN-LIKE REFINED ANALYSIS (separating AGN from SF by sigma):

WHAN AGN (logSFRHa > 0, sigma > 80): N=151, M/L = 1.53
Strong SF (logSFRHa > 0, sigma < 80): N=555, M/L = 0.18
Reference (logSFRHa in [-1.5, -0.5]): N=904, M/L = 0.52

WHAN AGN vs Reference: +197% (BUT this is a self-fulfilling signal because
M_dyn ∝ sigma², and high-sigma galaxies have high M_dyn by construction)

The narrow AGN cut (logSFRHa > 0.5, N=835) at low mass is the closest
proxy for "true AGN-like" sample, but is still contaminated by strong SF.

CAVEATS (CRITICAL):
1. logSFRHa is a NOISY AGN indicator. Many "AGN-like" galaxies are
   actually strong SF galaxies. Without BPT ([OIII]/Hbeta vs [NII]/Halpha)
   or X-ray detection, we cannot cleanly separate AGN from SF.
2. Vdispsc is dominated by stellar disk dynamics for late-type galaxies,
   not pure DM halo. The proper DM indicator for late-type is rotation
   velocity (Vrot), not velocity dispersion.
3. Morphology is a major confound. A proper test would match AGN hosts
   to control galaxies at fixed M* AND fixed morphology (e.g., only
   compare late-type AGN to late-type non-AGN).
4. The sample size of confirmed AGN is too small for robust statistics.
5. The +5% cascade prediction is small compared to the morphology effect.

CONCLUSION: INCONCLUSIVE.

The current test cannot distinguish cascade from ΛCDM because:
- The test is dominated by morphology confound
- The cascade's +5% prediction is below the morphology effect (~30%)
- The AGN classification is too noisy (no BPT available)

A definitive test would require:
1. BPT-classified AGN (N > 1000)
2. Morphology-matched control (matched on Hubble type, not just M*)
3. Vrot measurements (not just sigma) for late-type galaxies
4. Ideally X-ray-confirmed AGN (removes SF contamination)

This is a CLEAR example of a test that COULD distinguish cascade from
ΛCDM, but requires better data than is easily accessible.

The cascade's prediction remains UNT TESTED, not FALSIFIED, by this analysis.
