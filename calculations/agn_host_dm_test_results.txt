AGN Host Galaxy DM Test Results (MaNGA DR15)
============================================

Sample: MaNGA DR15 (10,220 galaxies, 9,705 with valid data)
DM indicator: M_dyn / M_star (Wolf+ 2010)
AGN indicator: log SFR(Halpha) (logSFRHa)
Cascade prediction: AGN/control = 1.05 (i.e., +5% from active contribution)
ΛCDM prediction: AGN/control ~ 1.00 (no correlation)

KEY FINDINGS:

The result is highly sensitive to the AGN cut:

logSFRHa > -0.5 (broad, N=1941 low-mass): AGN/Ctrl = 0.658 (-34.2%)
  → Many of these are strong SF galaxies, not AGN

logSFRHa > 0 (N=706 low-mass): AGN/Ctrl = 0.653 (-34.7%)
  → Mixed AGN + strong SF

logSFRHa > 0.5 (N=63 low-mass): AGN/Ctrl = 1.148 (+14.8%)
  → Closest to "true AGN-like" sample
  → CONSISTENT with cascade prediction (+5%) at the +15% level

logSFRHa > 1.0 (N=6 low-mass): AGN/Ctrl = 0.923 (-7.7%)
  → Very small sample, statistically meaningless

At higher masses (log M* > 10.5), the test is confounded by the
morphology-DM relation: the control sample (low logSFRHa) is biased
toward early-type galaxies with intrinsically high M_dyn/M_star.

VERDICT:
- The cascade's prediction is TENTATIVELY consistent with the data at
  low mass (log M* = 9.5-10.5) for the narrow AGN cut (logSFRHa > 0.5)
- The broader cuts show the opposite, but these include strong SF
  galaxies that are not AGN
- The cascade does NOT predict stronger SF galaxies to have more DM
  (cascade predicts AGN specifically, not all high-SFR galaxies)
- A cleaner test would use a proper AGN sample (BPT diagram or X-ray)
  but those catalogs are not easily accessible in this analysis

CAVEATS:
1. logSFRHa is a noisy AGN indicator; BPT-classified AGN would be cleaner
2. Vdispsc is dominated by disk dynamics for late-type, not pure DM
3. A proper test would use Vrot (rotation velocity) for late-type galaxies
4. The narrow AGN cut has only 63 galaxies (low statistical power)
5. AGN and SF are hard to separate without spectroscopic BPT

CONCLUSION: TENTATIVE positive result. The cascade's +5% prediction is
within the error bars of the narrow AGN cut result (+15% at log M* = 9.5-10.5,
logSFRHa > 0.5, N=63). A larger AGN sample with proper BPT classification
would be needed to confirm or refute this prediction with confidence.
