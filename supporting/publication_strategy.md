## Key framings for any public-facing communication

These are the v2.0 framings that should be consistent across all venues:

1. **"Bulk-brane cancellation"** + **"downward inversion"** — gravity is weak because of bulk-brane cancellation between the 4D event (bulk) and 3+1D universe (brane). The model claims that *downward* projection (parent → child universe) inverts the sign of gravity, but *upward* back-projection (child → parent) does not invert. The inversion is *directional*.

2. **"Two dark-sector products"** — dark energy is the un-cancelled fraction of the 4D→3+1D *downward* inversion (one inversion, repulsive). Dark matter is the *upward* back-projection of 2D universes' net attractive residue (no upward inversion, attractive). They look different because they're at different cascade levels and go in different directions.

3. **"Death-flash mechanism"** — dark matter is the *time-compressed Big Crunch* of child universes, not just any gravitational back-projection. Each child universe lives, evolves, and dies. The death-flash is the active source of dark matter.

4. **"Five possible endings"** — the model is intentionally ending-agnostic. All five (fixed-time boundary, cyclic, diminishing cyclic, Big Rip, Big Freeze) are consistent with the model and are empirically distinguishable by Euclid, Roman, LSST, and SKA.

5. **"D-labels are placeholders"** — "2D universe" doesn't mean "two spacetime dimensions." It means "one level down in the hierarchy." All universes are 3+1D spacetime.

6. **"AI-assisted thought experiment"** — full disclosure of AI involvement is in `ai_disclosure.md`. The AI was a tool; the conceptual content is the human author's.

## What NOT to do

- **Don't claim the model is "right" or "verified."** It's a thought experiment.
- **Don't oversell the math.** The "Mathematical sketch" in §2.4 is qualitative. No Lagrangian, no equations of motion. Be honest about this.
- **Don't oversell the predictions.** The activity-dependence prediction is interesting but not yet tested. The five endings are testable *in principle* by future observatories.
- **Don't post without acknowledging AI involvement.** Every venue increasingly requires this.
- **Don't ignore community norms about self-theories.** Reddit, in particular, will reject amateur theories regardless of merit.

## Reddit reality check (read this before posting anywhere)

Most physics subreddits are *gatekept* against amateur theories:

- **r/AskPhysics** — "No pseudoscientific self-theories"
- **r/TheoreticalPhysics** — "No self-theories allowed" (explicit ban)
- **r/Physics** — more lenient but still skeptical; amateur theories are usually downvoted
- **r/HypotheticalPhysics** — designed for new hypotheses, BUT has rules against AI-generated content
- **r/cosmology** — more focused, less amateur traffic

**Net**: Reddit is not a friendly venue for this kind of work. If posting to Reddit anyway, frame as a *question* ("What am I missing in this approach?") not a *proposal* ("Look at my theory").

## AI content rules across platforms
