# Publication Strategy Notes

## Reddit / forum reality check

Most physics subreddits are *gatekept* against amateur theories:

- **r/AskPhysics** — "No pseudoscientific self-theories"
- **r/TheoreticalPhysics** — "No self-theories allowed" (explicit ban)
- **r/Physics** — more lenient but still skeptical; amateur theories are usually downvoted
- **r/HypotheticalPhysics** — designed for new hypotheses, BUT has rules against AI-generated content
- **r/cosmology** — more focused, less amateur traffic

## AI content rules

Many platforms now have explicit rules about AI-generated content:

- **r/HypotheticalPhysics** — "P6: Acknowledge AI: Heavily AI-formatted posts will be removed. If your post includes minor content generated by AI tools..."
- **arXiv** (as of 2026) — banned AI hallucination citations for 1 year; explicit verification of references required
- **General principle** — be transparent about AI involvement

## Recommended path

### 1. Personal blog / Medium / Substack (PRIMARY)
- Write the layman summary as a personal essay
- Be upfront: "I developed this model in conversation with an AI assistant"
- Link to the full paper
- This is the *cleanest* path — no gatekeepers, no community rules
- The essay is your own intellectual contribution, the AI was a tool

### 2. LessWrong / EA Forum (SECONDARY)
- Long-form essay platforms where thoughtful speculation is welcome
- LessWrong in particular has a culture of "rationalist" speculation that fits this kind of work
- AI-assisted content is normal and expected

### 3. r/HypotheticalPhysics (TERTIARY, with caveats)
- Designed for new hypotheses
- BUT you need to:
  - Acknowledge AI involvement
  - Avoid "heavily AI-formatted" presentation (don't make it look too polished)
  - Engage as a "thought experiment" not a "theory"
  - Be ready for pushback

### 4. r/Physics (QUATERNARY, only as a question)
- Don't post as "look at my theory"
- Post as "is this kind of approach viable?" or "what am I missing?"
- Frame it as a question, not a proposal

## What NOT to do

- Don't post the full paper to Reddit. It's too long, too technical, and the gatekeepers will reject it.
- Don't post without acknowledging AI involvement. Many platforms now require this.
- Don't claim the model is "right" or "verified." It's a thought experiment.
- Don't ignore community rules about self-theories.

## If you do post to Reddit

- Lead with: "I'm a software engineer, not a physicist"
- Acknowledge: "I developed this in conversation with an AI assistant"
- Frame as: "What am I missing?" not "Look at my theory"
- Link to: a personal blog post with the full version, not the raw paper
- Be ready for: skeptical questions, dismissive comments, requests for derivation

## Bottom line

The paper is *yours* — you made the conceptual decisions, the AI was a tool. The community norms are evolving about AI-assisted work. The cleanest path is a personal blog/essay platform with full transparency about the process. Reddit is a secondary option, with appropriate framing.
